Dylan Hadfield-Menell

After completing his bachelor's and master's degrees at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, Dylan is now pursuing a PhD at Berkeley. He is working with Pieter Abbeel and Stuart Russell and is particularly interested in the area of integrated task and motion planning for robotics. In his spare time, he enjoys cooking and playing ultimate frisbee.

Alex Lee

Alex received his B.S. in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science from UC Berkeley and is now a first year Ph.D. student in Computer Science at Berkeley, working with Pieter Abbeel. His research interests are in Robotics and Computer Vision. In the past he has worked on belief space planning and tracking deformable objects. In his free time, Alex enjoys flying RC aircrafts, working on embedded device projects, and baking.

Robert Nishihara

Robert is broadly interested in machine learning and optimization and in the application of these tools to problems in the sciences. He majored in mathematics at Harvard University as an undergraduate.

Eric Tzeng

Eric Tzeng is a first year Ph.D. student at UC Berkeley working with Trevor Darrell. Eric studied at UC Berkeley for undergrad and graduated with a B.S. in EECS in 2013. Eric's research interests center around deep learning and its applications for object recognition. When not working on research, Eric enjoys board games, cooking, and puzzle hunts.

Colorado Reed

Colorado Reed's research background is in machine learning, experimental high-energy physics, and astrophysics. He completed an MPhil with Zoubin Ghahramani at the University of Cambridge and a BSc in applied physics from the University of Iowa. Colorado is supported by the NDSEG fellowship. Colorado is currently interested in developing tools that further personalize education, an interest that spans machine learning, HCI, and systems design.

Sandy Huang

Sandy received her B.S. in computer science from Stanford University. She is now a first year Ph.D. student in computer science at UC Berkeley, working with Pieter Abbeel. Her research interests lie at the intersection of machine learning and robotics. In her spare time, Sandy enjoys baking, board games, and outreach.

Shubham Tulsiani

Shubham completed his undergraduate studies at the Indian Institute of Technology, Kanpur. His primary interests are in the area of Computer Vision. He has worked on problems of Object and Activity Recognition and modeling human visual attention for detection. In his spare time, he follows soccer and plays table tennis.

Chi Jin

Chi Jin is a first Ph.D student in the EECS department at UC Berkeley working with Michael Jordan. Previously, he received his bachelor of science degree from Peking University where he majored in Physics. He also visited the machine learning group at University of Toronto for one year directly after his graduation. His previous work includes research in PAC-Bayes theory and differential privacy. His current research interests is generally on both statistical and computational learning theory, nonparametrics, and the theoretical challenge of big data.

Philipp Moritz

Philipp is a first-year PhD student in the EECS department at the University of California, Berkeley. He graduated with a BS in physics from the University of Wuerzburg in Germany. He then completed Part III of the Mathematical Tripos at the University of Cambridge, where he specialized in high-dimensional statistics and optimization. He is broadly interested in statistical machine learning and its applications to technology and science. His current research interests lie in developing mathematical theory and algorithms for machine learning.

Ahmed El Alaoui

Ahmed is a PhD student in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science (EECS) at UC Berkeley. His research interests are statistical learning theory, convex optimization and game theory. He received his bachelor in applied mathematics with an emphasis on stochastic calculus from Ecole Polytechnique (France) in 2012, and his MS in machine learning from Ecole Normale Sup√©rieure in 2013. He worked on several machine learning, estimation, and control problems, both in academic and industrial contexts, which include: convex relaxations for probabilistic record linkage and pricing of financial assets.

Ali Punjani

Ali graduated in 2012 from the University of Toronto, where he studied Engineering Science - Aerospace. In the past, he's done work in MRI techniques, unmanned aviation, photogrammetry, large scale nearest neighbor and image retrieval, molecular imaging and reconstruction, audio and speech processing, circuit design and 3D printing. His current interests continue to evolve, but include computer vision for biomedical applications, large scale machine learning techniques, and technological approaches to social change. In his spare time he likes to volunteer as a tutor, fly radio control planes, program CNC machines, and meditate.

Lisa Anne Hendricks

Lisa Anne Hendricks is interested in computer vision, especially the intersection of machine learning and vision. At UC Berkeley, she is working with Professor Trevor Darrell. Before arriving at UC Berkeley, she graduated from Rice University with a degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering with an emphasis on signal processing. At Rice, she researched imaging applications of compressed sensing with Professor Richard Baraniuk and Professor Ashok Veeraraghavan. She interned with the Chrome team at Google during the summer of 2012 and with the Glass team at Google during the the summer of 2013.

Ting-Chun Wang

Ting-Chun Wang is a first-year PhD student in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science at UC Berkeley. He graduated with a BS degree in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science from National Taiwan University in 2012. His research interests include image processing, computer vision, computational photography and computer graphics.

Constantin Berzan

Constantin is a first-year graduate student currently advised by Stuart Russell. He is interested in active learning and lifelong learning, with applications to vision and robotics. He did his undergraduate studies at Tufts University, where his senior thesis topic was learning structure in Bayesian networks.

Tinghui Zhou

Tinghui is currently a PhD student in the EECS department of UC Berkeley, where he works on large-scale object recognition and image understanding. Before joining Berkeley, he obtained a Masters degree from the Robotics Institute at Carnegie Mellon University. He did his undergraduate study at University of Minnesota majoring in computer science.
